Before entering SOC

After secondary school, I managed to enter Singapore Polytechnic through PFP (Polytechnic Foundation Programme). I decided to enter PFP as I didn't want to enter ITE, and wanted to have an achievement in my life. I felt happy that I manage to enter because I didn't want the hard work I spent on studying for my N-Levels to go to waste.

The one year I spent in PFP gave me the chance to join CCAs that I was interested in, such as SP Fencing. I made many friends in Fencing through the training sessions and we would go to the Each A Cup that was located at Dover MRT after training sessions to get bubbble tea.

The one year in PFP also gave me the chance to experience how polytechnic life was going to be,. It was hard adjusting at first as I have to wake up at different timings for the different timings my classes started back then, but over time I slowly got used to it. I also made new friends in my PFP class and even though we are now in different courses, we still keep in contact.

What is PFP?

PFP (Polytechnic Foundation Programme) is a one year programme that is for the top 10% of N-Level students. Instead of going to Secondary 5 and do O-Levels, they can enter SP through PFP and get a provisional place in their respective course. Before they go into their diploma, they have to complete a series of modules and pass them in order to progress to to their respective diplomas.
